Julia Proft

Welcome! I'm a PhD candidate in computer science at Cornell University, and I'm rethinking how computational thinking is taught. More specifically, I am developing an adaptive learning system that teaches problem-solving skills by having students program a robot. I'm interested in how the system can spark intrinsic motivation in students, support their mastery of computational concepts, increase their scientific and technological self-efficacy, and challenge them at an appropriate level. I evaluate my system in real-world settings, with the ultimate goal of optimizing it for learners in both classrooms and online courses.

Outside of my research, I also have the privilege of being a computer science instructor. Locally, I co-facilitate a Girls Who Code program where I get to share stories about inspiring women in technology and coach aspiring women in technology. Globally, I teach Python, JavaScript, and more to Juni Learning students who always brighten my day.
